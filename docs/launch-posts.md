## r/interactivefiction
**Title:** I adapted the entire Divine Comedy (Inferno AND Purgatorio) into a
branching game where Hell remembers you between playthroughs

**Body:**
For the past stretch I've been building NINE CIRCLES — a two-act
branching-narrative game adapted from Dante, using Longfellow's 1867
translation (the actual verses are collectible items, and one of them is the
only key that makes Ugolino stop gnawing and tell you about the tower).

Mechanics I'm proudest of:
- You can carry **three names** per descent. The damned beg to be remembered;
  names only inscribe on the Witness Wall if you exit alive. Nine specific
  souls across many runs open the true path — the ninth is your guide.
- **The game lies to you when your grace runs low.** Choice text visibly
  corrupts, and the stars you exit under might be painted on stone.
- **Hell remembers.** Souls recognize returning pilgrims by your last verdict
  on them. In Act II you can drink Lethe and erase their grudges permanently.
- **Act II inverts the whole design**: sins become burdens you shed, scarce
  names become weightless prayers, the star becomes the sun, and the guide who
  carried you through nine circles cannot follow you through the fire.

Free in the browser, no install: https://kylefriesmarketing.github.io/nine-circles/
Feedback very welcome — especially from anyone who reaches "Pure and Disposed."
