# NINE CIRCLES — itch.io page copy

## Title
NINE CIRCLES — a descent (and the mountain after)

## Short description (itch "short description" field)
A branching journey through Dante's Inferno and Purgatorio. Carry three names
through the dark — they inscribe only if you leave alive. Then carry prayers
up the mountain. Two acts, 35 endings.

## Page body

**You wake midway upon the journey of your life, in a wood you never entered.**
A dead Roman poet offers you the only road out: down. Through all nine circles —
past Francesca's storm, Ugolino's ice, and the frozen Emperor at the bottom of
the world — and, if you keep your star lit, out the far side to see the stars again.

**Hell reads you.** Seven sins accrue from your choices, and the circles tailor
their temptations to your ledger. Deadly options *gleam* when a floor matches
your appetite. Minos remembers your past descents. So do the souls.

**The dead ask one thing: to be remembered.** You can carry three names per
descent. They are inscribed on the Witness Wall only if you make it out alive.
Nine souls, remembered across many descents — the ninth is not who you expect —
open the way past the stars.

**And then: the mountain.** Act II inverts everything. The sins you walked down
with become the burdens you climb off, terrace by terrace. The dead no longer
beg to be remembered — they beg you to carry prayers UP to the living, and
prayers weigh nothing. Climb by day. Dream by night. Beware the Siren. And at
the wall of fire, learn what your guide cannot do.

### Features
- **Two full acts** — the descent (Inferno) and the ascent (Purgatorio)
- **35 endings**, from joining the whirlwind to taking Death's job to the
  mountain's own last line
- **Verses as keys**: real tercets from Longfellow's 1867 translation are
  collectible wards — one of them is the only thing that makes Ugolino speak
- **A grace meter that lies**: when Beatrice's Star gutters, the game's own
  text begins to corrupt
- **Punish or Absolve** the famous damned — wrestle Death for his scythe in
  the first act's opening minutes, if you dare
- **Hell remembers**: souls greet returning pilgrims by your last verdict on
  them; drinking Lethe at the summit erases their grievances forever
- Fully illustrated painted scenes, illuminated soul medallions, and a
  generative score whose bells decay note-by-note with your grace
- Runs in any browser; nothing to install; saves locally
